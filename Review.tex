\chapter{Literature Review}
\vspace{-1cm}
The paradigm shift toward Automated Machine Learning (AutoML) and robust MLOps frameworks has redefined the benchmarks for modern software engineering and artificial intelligence. This literature survey investigates the strategic intersection where no-code simplicity meets heavy-duty industrial automation, formalizing a framework for high-scale, resilient AI deployment. By dissecting advanced methodologies in automated exploratory data analysis, dynamic feature synthesis, and hyperparameter optimization, the coming analysis maps the essential infrastructure needed to translate high-dimensional data science into intuitive, user-oriented digital products. This investigation consolidates insights from pioneering academic works to validate the architecture of a modular, transparent, and autonomous ecosystem.

The theoretical core of any AutoML system lies in its ability to navigate a high-dimensional search space through intelligent optimization strategies. Foundational research by Hutter et al. (2019) suggests that by automating the data preprocessing and feature engineering stages, the likelihood of discovering high-performance models increases significantly while simultaneously reducing human-induced bias. This study examines how search strategies have evolved to provide more efficient pathways for hyperparameter tuning. Understanding these core principles is essential for developing a platform that remains computationally efficient while exploring a wide range of algorithmic possibilities.\vspace{0.4cm}

Beyond the initial induction of a model, the focus of current academic discourse has shifted toward the sustainability of AI through MLOps. The integration of continuous development and automated deployment pipelines ensures that machine learning systems remain resilient in production environments. As noted by Kreuzberger et al. (2022), the integration of containerization ensures that models perform reliably regardless of the underlying infrastructure. This review analyzes the methodologies for monitoring model performance and detecting data drift, which are critical for maintaining the accuracy of automated systems over their entire operational lifecycle.\vspace{0.4cm}

The crystallization of these theories into practical tools is seen in the work of Feurer et al. (2015) and Olson and Moore (2016), who introduced robust frameworks like Auto-sklearn and TPOT. These systems demonstrate that genetic programming and Bayesian optimization can effectively automate the complex task of pipeline discovery. By abstracting the underlying mathematical and programmatic complexity, these tools empower domain experts to build custom solutions without traditional coding barriers. This investigation explores how no-code frameworks can maintain high standards of reproducibility and efficiency, ensuring that the results generated by these systems are both trustworthy and actionable for a non-technical audience.\vspace{0.4cm}

\section{Comparative Analysis of Literature Review}

Automated Machine Learning (AutoML) represents a significant shift in reducing the manual burden of designing and evaluating complex models. A foundational work by Hutter et al. (2019) in ``Automated Machine Learning: Methods, Systems, Challenges,'' provides a deep dive into the transition from basic hyperparameter tuning to holistic end-to-end automation. This research categorizes critical lifecycle stages including automated feature extraction and intelligent algorithm selection. A key finding is the persistent lack of unified, user-friendly interfaces that allow non-experts to leverage these high-level technical processes. This gap serves as the primary technical motivation for the development of InferX ML. [1]\vspace{0.4cm}

The survey conducted by He et al. (2021) in ``AutoML: A Survey of the State-of-the-Art'' provides a comprehensive analysis of various techniques used in the industry to automate neural architecture search and hyperparameter optimization. This study validates that modern AutoML techniques are now capable of matching human experts in specific data science tasks, provided the underlying search strategy is sufficiently robust. InferX ML builds upon these findings by offering a diverse set of algorithmic options that are automatically optimized for cross-validation performance. [2]\vspace{0.4cm}

The recent evolution and future trends of the field are mapped by Baratchi et al. (2024) in ``Automated Machine Learning: Past, Present and Future.'' This study identifies the growing need for Explainable AI (XAI) within automated systems to build trust among stakeholders. It emphasizes that the future of AutoML lies in making the "black box" decisions more transparent. In alignment with this research, InferX ML has been designed to provide clear visualizations of model logic and feature importance to ensure that every prediction is fully understandable and justifiable. [3]\vspace{0.4cm}

Feurer et al. (2015) in ``Efficient and Robust Automated Machine Learning'' introduced the Auto-sklearn framework, which marked a breakthrough in using meta-learning to initialize hyperparameter search. By leveraging past performance on similar datasets, the system can converge on optimal configurations significantly faster. InferX ML applies these principles of efficiency to ensure that users can train high-accuracy models without needing extensive computational resources or time-consuming manual intervention. [4]\vspace{0.4cm}

The use of genetic programming for pipeline discovery is explored by Olson and Moore (2016) in their discussion of TPOT (Tree-Based Pipeline Optimization Tool). This research demonstrates that machine learning pipelines can be evolved dynamically to find the best possible combination of preprocessors and model types. This dynamic approach to automation is reflected in the modular architecture of InferX ML, which allows for the seamless integration of various data cleaning and feature engineering steps into a single, cohesive workflow. [5]\vspace{0.4cm}

Zöller and Huber (2021) provided a detailed benchmark in ``Benchmark and Survey of Automated Machine Learning Frameworks,'' highlighting the performance trade-offs between different AutoML libraries. Their analysis shows that while many tools achieve high accuracy, they often lack the portability required for enterprise deployment. This reinforces the decision to use containerization in InferX ML, ensuring that the platform remains consistently performant across different local and cloud-based environments. [6]\vspace{0.4cm}

The architectural requirements for managing these systems are formalised by Kreuzberger et al. (2022) in their work on MLOps. This research defines the critical need for automated versioning, continuous training, and monitoring to maintain model integrity in production. InferX ML incorporates these MLOps principles by providing a centralized database for model management and using asynchronous task queues to handle resource-intensive training jobs reliably over time. [7]\vspace{0.4cm}

Reproducibility remains a core challenge in the field, as discussed by Gundersen and Kjensmo (2018) in ``State of the Art: Reproducibility in Artificial Intelligence.'' They argue that for AI results to be credible, every stage of the pipeline must be fully auditable. This emphasizes the importance of the logging and storage systems implemented in the InferX ML backend, which ensure that every training session and prediction is recorded with its associated metadata for future reference and verification. [8]\vspace{0.4cm}

Finally, Vanschoren (2018) in ``Meta-Learning: A Survey'' explores how history-driven automation can optimize the selection of new algorithms. By analyzing the characteristics of an input dataset, the system can suggest candidates with the highest probability of success. InferX ML leverages these meta-learning concepts to guide its internal selection engine, providing users with a high-performance baseline from the very first interaction with the platform. [9]\vspace{0.4cm}
